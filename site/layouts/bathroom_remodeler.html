Timeline

When you are doing a bathroom project, it will take some time. The timeline for the project will vary depending on what you are doing.

This is a general idea of how the project should happen:
 The contractor and customer meet to discuss goals and objectives
 A plan is created for what needs to be done
 In the planning phase, you are getting permits and making changes to your home's floor plan. You might need to change the layout because of plumbing or something else. The planning phase is usually 2 weeks. Don't forget to factor in holidays and weekends.
 Demolition phase can take anywhere from 5-10 days. It may be longer or shorter depending on what you are remodeling.

Framework phase

This is when the contractor will frame your new bathroom. It should only take 1-2 days to do this, depending on how big and complicated the project is.

 In the smoke and rework phase, there will be more changes. These may happen because something was not seen before or because of a change in materials.
 Time for the glazing phase. We will be finishing the electrical, plumbing, tiling, and fixing any last-minute changes.
 Anticipate a week or two for the glazing phase once everything is set.
